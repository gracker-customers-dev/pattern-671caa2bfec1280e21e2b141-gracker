Introduction

Understanding Threat Prevention

Types of Threats

Cybersecurity threats come in various forms, each with unique characteristics and implications. The most common types include:

  • Malware: Malicious software designed to harm or exploit any programmable device, service, or network.
  • Phishing Attacks: Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information by disguising as a trustworthy entity.
  • Insider Threats: Risks posed by individuals within the organization who may misuse their access for malicious purposes.

Role of Monitoring Tools

Monitoring tools are vital in threat prevention. They provide capabilities for:

  • Data Collection: Continuous gathering of data from various sources to identify patterns and anomalies.
  • Anomaly Detection: Identifying deviations from established baselines to detect potential threats.
  • Incident Response: Facilitating quick and effective reactions to security incidents, minimizing damage.
